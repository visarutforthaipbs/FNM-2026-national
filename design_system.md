As a civic tech project, the goal is "Industrial Transparency." Here is a proposed design system and branding strategy for 2026.1. Brand Identity & ConceptBrand Persona: The "Honest Inspector." Reliable, objective, and clear.Core Value: “Bringing the factory walls down through data.”Logo Concept: A minimalist factory silhouette (the traditional "sawtooth" roof) integrated with a location pin or a magnifying glass.Thai Tagline: "เปิดข้อมูลโรงงาน เพื่อชุมชนที่น่าอยู่" (Opening factory data for a livable community).2. Color Palette (The "Industrial-Eco" Scheme)We avoid "Warning Red" as a primary color to prevent unnecessary panic. Instead, we use colors that suggest monitoring and environmental health.CategoryColor NameHex CodePurposePrimaryIndustrial Blue#1A365DDeep, professional blue for headers and trust.SecondarySafety Orange#F59E0BFor "Action" items like Search buttons or Warning icons.AccentEco Green#10B981To highlight "Green Industry" ratings or clean records.NeutralConcrete Gray#F3F4F6Backgrounds and cards to keep the UI clean.Data: RiskAlert Crimson#EF4444Only for high-risk pollution or closed factories.3. Typography: "The Clear Communicator"Since you are dealing with complex data and Thai script, legibility is the highest priority.Primary Font (Thai): IBM Plex Sans Thai. It is modern, professional, and has excellent readability for long lists of company names and addresses.Secondary Font (English/Numbers): Inter or Roboto. Perfect for the 13-digit Juristic IDs and technical data points.4. Design System ComponentsTo make the data "easy to see by ordinary people," we use a Card-Based UI rather than a table-heavy UI.The "Factory Card":Top: Factory Name & Distance (e.g., 2.4 km away).Body: Status Badge (Active/Inactive) and Industry Type (TSIC Icon).Footer: "Human Side" snippet (Owned by: [Company Name]).The "Impact Pulse":A small sparkline or color-coded dot showing the latest pollution/complaint status. 🟢 (Clean), 🟡 (Reported), 🔴 (Incident).5. Interaction & Experience (UX)The "Proximity First" Search: The homepage should automatically ask for location to show "Factories within 5km" immediately.Layered Complexity: * Level 1: Casual user sees the map and factory names.Level 2: Interested neighbor clicks to see "Who owns this?" (DBD data).Level 3: Pro user/Researcher downloads the full R.N.4 permit data.🏗️ Design System ChecklistAccessibility (WCAG 2.1): Ensure all text has at least a 4.5:1 contrast ratio. In 2026, Thai users often use "Dark Mode"—ensure your map tiles (Mapbox/Google) swap to a dark theme automatically.Iconography: Use a custom icon set for factory types (e.g., a "Gear" for manufacturing, a "Drop" for chemical, a "Wheat" for food processing). This helps people identify factories at a glance without reading.Mobile-First: 80% of your users will likely access this via a smartphone while standing in their neighborhood.
